## ADDED Requirements

### Requirement: First-Viewport Credibility Claim
The homepage SHALL make the first viewport credible to a serious product or startup reader by clearly stating what Dushyant builds, who the work serves, and why the work is grounded in practical product judgment.

#### Scenario: Visitor scans the hero
- **WHEN** a first-time visitor scans the homepage hero for no more than ten seconds
- **THEN** the hero communicates Dushyant's Product Manager / Builder role
- **AND** identifies practical AI products and product systems as the work focus
- **AND** names the operational/user context without relying on vague founder-brand language

#### Scenario: Hero copy avoids overclaiming
- **WHEN** hero copy, CTAs, or adjacent proof text render
- **THEN** the copy uses objective, concrete product language
- **AND** does not use hype, fake metrics, self-mythologizing, or unverifiable claims

### Requirement: Intentional Identity Visual
The homepage SHALL treat the identity portrait as a credibility cue only when it supports the serious first impression.

#### Scenario: Portrait is retained
- **WHEN** the homepage includes a portrait in the first viewport
- **THEN** the image is sized, cropped, and positioned as a deliberate professional identity element
- **AND** it does not distract from the positioning claim or read as a casual decorative sticker

#### Scenario: Portrait cannot support the intended tone
- **WHEN** the available portrait does not support the serious Product Manager / Builder positioning
- **THEN** the hero de-emphasizes or removes the portrait rather than forcing it into the first viewport
